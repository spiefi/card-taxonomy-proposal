Card taxonomy proposal

3 reusable cards, not 10 bespoke cards.

The survey looks noisy because media, badges, CTAs, and density keep changing. The underlying behaviors are stable: commercial offer display, commercial offer action, and editorial/native content.

3 public card components
5 shared composables
0 use-case variants

Recommended Public API

Split by behavior and information hierarchy. Do not split by image versus logo, badge count, sponsorship text, or card size.

OfferCard

Merchant reward display card. It sells the offer but does not own reveal-code or activation state.

Covers

  • Regular cashback merchant tiles
  • Top-up bonus cards
  • Logo-only stacked merchant cards
  • Image-plus-logo sponsored cards
  • Favorite + activate merchant card, via action slot

Core props

  • density: hero, default, compact, rail
  • mediaKind: logo, image, imageWithLogo, none
  • merchant, title, subtitle
  • badges, meta, sponsored

Slots

  • leading: merchant logo or badge stack
  • action: Button, favorite CTA, or none
  • footer: expiry, sponsorship, conditions

ActionOfferCard

Offer card with a conversion state machine: activate cashback, reveal code, placeholder, expiry, and conditions.

Covers

  • Amazon + iGraal activation card
  • Verified discount code card
  • Acer cashback + code/deal card
  • Any card where action state changes the body

Core props

  • offerType: cashback, code, cashbackCode, bonus
  • actionState: locked, activated, revealed, expired
  • partnerLogo, merchantLogo, title
  • legalLink, validity, verification

Slots

  • rewardSummary: rate/badge cluster
  • actionArea: CTA, CodeReveal, placeholder
  • footer: conditions and expiry

MediaCard

Image-dominant editorial or native ad card. The image is the subject, not decoration around a merchant offer.

Covers

  • Summer editorial card
  • MSI / Nike / MediaMarkt content cards
  • Sponsored native content rows
  • Campaign article cards with arrow affordance

Core props

  • orientation: vertical, horizontal
  • aspectRatio: 16:9, 4:3, 1:1
  • source, headline, body
  • logo, badge, sponsored

Slots

  • mediaOverlay: badge or label
  • trailing: arrow, none, or small CTA

Shared Composables

These keep the three-card model from turning into prop soup. They should be documented as the expected slot contents.

Build these once

  • CardShell: surface, radius, border, padding, elevation, overflow.
  • MerchantIdentity: logo, merchant name, co-brand pairing.
  • BadgeStack: cashback, code, verified, expiry, top-up bonus.
  • CodeReveal: placeholder, reveal state, copied state, expired state.
  • OfferFooter: sponsored, expiry, conditions link, legal copy.

Variable modes

  • Density: hero, default, compact, rail.
  • Appearance: neutral, primary, secondary, sparkle.
  • Emphasis: default, subtle, ghost.
  • Platform: screen-level only; never scoped to the card.
  • Color Mode: screen-level only; the card consumes output tokens.

The key rule: when a new card asks for another boolean prop, first try a slot composable. New props should describe stable structure, not one campaign's temporary badge arrangement.

What Not To Combine

These boundaries are what keep the system reusable instead of becoming one god-card with thirty props.

Do not merge MediaCard into OfferCard

Editorial cards are image-first. Offer cards are merchant/rate/action-first. Forcing both into one API creates ambiguous slots and inconsistent list behavior.

Do not make density separate components

OfferCardCompact and OfferCardHero would duplicate the same contract. Density should be token-driven.

Do not encode every badge as a boolean

Use a composed BadgeStack. Props like isTopUpBonus, isVerified, and hasExpiryChip will rot fast.

Do not bury business logic in OfferCard

Reveal code, activate cashback, favorite, and copy states belong to action composables or containers, not the presentational card shell.

Risk Decision
OfferCard becoming a god component Keep action behavior in slot composables. New campaign behavior should not add another OfferCard prop by default.
Unstable heights in rails and grids Lock image ratios and line clamps per density. Rail cards should not accept arbitrary body copy.
